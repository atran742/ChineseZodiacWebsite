What is you Chinese Zodiac?

The 12 lucky animals

The Year of the Dragon: 1940, 1952, 1964, 1976, 1988, 2000, 2024
As the symbol of Chinese culture, the dragon represents authority and good fortune. People born in the Year of the Dragon are powerful, kind-hearted, successful, innovative, brave, fearless, healthy courageous and enterprising.
The Year of the Rabbit: 1939, 1951, 1963, 1975, 1987, 1999, 2011, 2023
The Rabbit represents longevity, discretion and good luck. It has the 4th position in the Chinese Zodiac. People born under the sign of the Rabbit are kind-hearted, friendly, intelligent, cautious, skilful, gentle, quick and live a very long life.
The Year of the Rat: 1936, 1948, 1960, 1972, 1984, 1996, 2008, 2020
Rats have excellent taste, are loyal friends and are generous to others considered part of its pack. Rats are also hard workers and have the best chance of being wealthy and successful in their career, out of all the zodiac signs.
The Year of the Ox: 1937, 1949, 1961, 1973, 1985, 1997, 2009, 2021
Ranked 2nd in the Chinese Zodiac, the Ox is the symbol of diligence in Chinese culture. People born in the Year of the Ox are hard workers and have a strong sense of responsibility. Even if they run into difficulty during work, they’ll persevere through.
The Year of the Tiger: 1938, 1950, 1962, 1974, 1986, 1998, 2010, 2022
Tigers are the symbol of the brave and are 3rd in the Zodiac. People born in the Year of the Tiger are friendly, competitive, charming and endowed with good fortune and authority. With indomitable fortitude and great confidence, tigers can be competent leaders, easily winning peoples’ trust.
The Year of the Snake: 1941, 1953, 1965, 1977, 1989, 2001, 2013, 2025
Among the Chinese Zodiac animals, the Snake holds 6th position in the Zodiac cycle. The Snake is regarded to be a symbol of wisdom and wit. Snakes are seductive, gregarious, introverted, generous, charming, and good with money. They rely on their gut feelings; are hard-working and intelligent.

These shining characteristics make them popular with people to have around.
The Year of the Pig: 1947, 1959, 1971, 1983, 1995, 2007, 2019, 2031

They enjoy helping others and are good companions.
